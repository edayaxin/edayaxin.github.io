Cocoon

Media: Oak Corns, Plaster, Tree Branches, Cloth

Oak corns in the cocoons are collected on the campus of Dartmouth College and echo my experience there as an exchange student in 2014. The tree of cocoons is a collection of memories. The sculpture course was my first art class in college and has motivated me to continue arts in the rest of my undergraduate journey.

Afterlife

Media: Charcoal, Tape

With blossom of charcoals, the installation is an imagined life form in the afterlife of a tree. It awaits the viewer's untroubled mind and contemplative gaze.





Tree of A Year

Media: Watercolor, Electronics, Computer Graphics, Projector

Four sides of the tree represent four seasons in a year. People walk around the tree as if they are experiencing the passage of time. It is a media art practice that seeks to create enchanted interaction between people and mundane objects.





Wind of Leaves

Media: Clay, Wire, Microsoft Kinetic, Computer Graphics, Projector

On the stage, leaves are composing a dance song for the spring. Their movement changes as clay models are put in different places on the stage. It is an experiment on interactive projecting with object tracking and computer graphics.
